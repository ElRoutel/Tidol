# DMCA Policy

**Last Updated:** June 29, 2026

## Overview

Tidol respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects its users to do the same. Tidol is a **search aggregator** — we do not host copyrighted audio content. All music is played through official platform embeds (YouTube, Spotify, SoundCloud) or from legally licensed Internet Archive collections.

## What Tidol Does and Does Not Host

### We Do NOT Host:
- Audio files from YouTube, Spotify, SoundCloud, or any copyrighted source
- Copies or caches of copyrighted music
- Unauthorized reproductions of protected works

### We DO Host:
- Music metadata (titles, artist names, album information) sourced from MusicBrainz (open data)
- Cover art links (pointing to original sources like iTunes/Cover Art Archive)
- User-generated data (playlists, listening history, preferences)
- Search indexes referencing publicly available metadata

## DMCA Takedown Procedure

If you believe that content accessible through Tidol infringes your copyright, please note:

1. **For content played via YouTube/Spotify/SoundCloud embeds:** Please file your takedown request directly with the hosting platform, as Tidol does not host or control this content.

2. **For Internet Archive content:** Internet Archive content served through Tidol is limited to Creative Commons and public domain licensed material. If you believe CC-licensed content has been incorrectly licensed, please contact the Internet Archive directly at info@archive.org.

3. **For metadata or search results:** If you believe that metadata displayed on Tidol (such as a title, artist name, or album cover link) infringes your rights, send a DMCA notice to:

**DMCA Agent**  
Email: dmca@tidol.app

Your notice must include:
- Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed
- Identification of the material that is claimed to be infringing, with sufficient detail to locate it
- Your contact information (name, address, email, phone)
- A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use is not authorized
- A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, under penalty of perjury
- Your physical or electronic signature

## Counter-Notification

If you believe that material was removed or disabled by mistake, you may file a counter-notification including:
- Identification of the removed material and its prior location
- A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed by mistake
- Your name, address, and phone number
- Consent to jurisdiction of the federal court in your district
- Your physical or electronic signature

## Repeat Infringers

Tidol will terminate access for users who are repeat infringers, in accordance with the DMCA.
